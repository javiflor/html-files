Vaccinations

We offer vaccinations, deworming, and microchipping services on a walk-in basis, during our vaccine clinic hours, at our Medical Center. No appointment is necessary.

*Prices of services subject to change without notice. Additional services are available at time of Spay/Neuter.

Canine Vaccinations and Services

These vaccine protocols are general guidelines. You should discuss your pet’s specific needs with your veterinarian.

Canine Influenza Virus H3N2/H3N8 (CIV) - $35
Starting at 8 weeks of age, then again 2 to 4 weeks later, then once annually.

DA2PP - $25
Starting at 6 to 8 weeks of age, every 3 to 4 weeks until 20 weeks of age, and then annually. Over 20 weeks of age, once, then annually.

Bordetella - $25
Starting at 8 weeks old, or older, to protect against kennel cough.

Rabies - $20
Must be at least 3 months (12 weeks) old. First vaccine is good for one year and from then on the vaccine will be valid for 3 years.

De-worming
$15 Puppy (under 20 weeks):
Roundworm/hookworm de-wormer recommended to be repeated in 3 weeks after initial dose.

Roundworm, Tapeworm, and Hookworm de-wormer:
$30 Dogs less than 25 pounds
$40 Dogs 26 - 60 pounds
$55 Dogs 61 pounds and over


Feline Vaccinations and Services

These vaccine protocols are general guidelines. You should discuss your pet’s specific needs with your veterinarian.

FVRCP - $25
Starting at 6 to 8 weeks of age, every 3 to 4 weeks until 20 weeks of age, and then annually. Over 20 weeks of age, two vaccines 3 to 4 weeks apart and then once annually.

Rabies - $20
Must be at least 3 months (12 weeks) old. First vaccine is good for one year and from then on the vaccine will be valid for 3 years.

FeLV (Feline Leukemia Virus) - $35
Recommended for all cats that go outside 12 weeks and older. Initial two vaccines given 3 weeks apart and then annually.

De-worming
$15 Kitten (under 20 weeks):
Roundworm/hookworm de-wormer recommended to be repeated in 3 weeks after initial dose.

$25 Cat Roundworm, Tapeworm, and Hookworm de-wormer


Microchipping

Protect your companion animal with nationwide coverage with a microchip. A permanent identification chip that is administered similar to a routine vaccine that allows your pet to be identified if lost.

$45 regular price
$25 at time of spay or neuter surgery

Commited to Making Things Right

The Paws N Tails Center was established in 2015 to serve the South Bay area. The PNTC offers pet adoptions, medical care services, pet training, and first-time pet owner education. At PNTC, we care about finding the right fit for the right owner.

Our mission is rescue lost and abandoned animals in our local community, assist animals by providing food, shelter and vet care to those in need, promoting spay and neutering to end the population of unwanted pets, and provide adoption opportunities of animals to loving homes.

Our vision to strive for a world where every animal has the medical help it needs to sustain longevity and a forever loving-home.

FAQs

Where do the pets come from?

We receive animals from individuals who are no longer able to care for their pets and from other shelters that lack placement resources in their area. We also provide adoption services to animals that are lost, brought in as strays, abandoned, or seized in humane investigation cases.

How do I meet a pet I’m interested in adopting?

You are welcome to visit the shelter anytime during adoption business hours to meet a pet you’re interested in adopting. Once at the shelter, staff and volunteers are available to assist you. All household/family members should interact with the pet before the adoption is finalized to ensure the pet is compatible with everyone.

How long will the adoption process take?

Plan to allow one to two hours to complete the adoption process. It may take longer depending on how many pets you wish to meet, your previous experience with pet care, and the number of other visitors at the shelter. Peak days for visitor traffic are Fridays, Saturdays, and Sundays, so please allow extra time if you plan to visit on those days. Adoptions can be processed up to 30 minutes before closing time. If you wish to adopt and it’s less than 30 minutes before closing, we can place the pet on hold for you to finish your visitation and/or finalize the adoption the following day.

What if I need more time to decide? Can I place a pet on hold?

A non-refundable, non-transferrable hold deposit may be placed on a pet for a 24-hour period. If the adoption is completed during that time, the deposit is applied toward the adoption fee. If the hold is cancelled or the adoption is not completed, the deposit is forfeited. Only one hold may be placed on a pet at a time. All adoptions and adoption holds must be processed in person.

Have more questions? Please email us at pawsntails@adoption.com

Commited to Making Things Right

The Paws N Tails Center was established in 2015 to serve the South Bay area. The PNTC offers pet adoptions, medical care services, pet training, and first-time pet owner education. At PNTC, we care about finding the right fit for the right owner.

Our mission is rescue lost and abandoned animals in our local community, assist animals by providing food, shelter and vet care to those in need, promoting spay and neutering to end the population of unwanted pets, and provide adoption opportunities of animals to loving homes.

Our vision to strive for a world where every animal has the medical help it needs to sustain longevity and a forever loving-home.
